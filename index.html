Meet the Souseman

Larry Reaves, better known as the Souseman, built a soul food legacy from the porch of his Opa-locka home. After turning his life around from years in the streets and addiction, he answered a spiritual calling that led to the recipe for his now-famous pork and chicken souse — a vinegary, citrusy stew deeply rooted in Black and Caribbean culture.

Featured on Netflix’s Street Food: USA, Souseman’s story has touched hearts around the world — and brought customers to his doorstep from as far as Switzerland. But the truth is, this journey started with one pot, one prayer, and a promise: make the souse the same way every time, and he’d never work for another man again.

Today, he serves souse, ribs, seafood rice, and full dinner plates with a side of ministry and hospitality — not from a big restaurant, but from the same front porch that made him famous. “To me,” he says, “it’s a staple of the culture.”

Stop by, grab a plate, and experience the food that changed his life — and might just bless yours too.
